# Budget Analytics Model

Date: 2026-05-15
Scope: Fixed budget analytical base for monthly planning, provision, realization, competence tracking, and deficit/superavit visibility
Status: Approved working model

## 1. Objective

Provide a practical analytical structure that supports:

- monthly budget by asset, cost center, and budget item
- monthly and annual visibility of approved, provisioned, realized, committed, and balance values
- competence-based financial control independent from payment date
- deficit and superavit visibility
- future migration from workbook operating model into portal data structures

## 2. Recommended Logical Model

The recommended operating model is split into structural master data plus monthly facts.

### 2.1 Structural Dimension

Canonical entity: one row per combination of:

- asset
- cost center
- budget item

Logical fields:

- `dimension_id`
- `tenant_id`
- `modality`
- `asset_id`
- `asset_name`
- `cost_center_code`
- `cost_center_name`
- `budget_item_code`
- `budget_item_name`
- `budget_group`
- `cost_nature`
- `active_flag`

### 2.2 Budget Plan Monthly

One row per:

- `dimension_id`
- `competence_year`

- `competence_year`
- `competence_month_number`
- `planned_amount`

## 3. Analytical Rules

### 3.1 Competence

- `competence_month` is the accounting month the expense belongs to.
- `payment_date` may happen in another month.
- Analysis must be primarily competence-based, not payment-month-based.

### 3.2 Approved Budget

- `approved_amount` is the official comparison baseline.
- `planned_amount` and `reforecast_amount` are planning layers.
- Deficit and superavit must always compare against `approved_amount`.

### 3.3 Provisioned, Realized, Committed

- `provisioned_open` = open obligations not yet paid
- `realized` = effectively paid values
- `committed` = `provisioned_open + realized`
- `balance` = `approved_amount - committed`

### 3.4 Deficit and Superavit

- `balance < 0` -> `DEFICIT`
- `balance > 0` -> `SUPERAVIT`
- `balance = 0` -> `ZERADO`

### 3.5 Allocation Rule

- `allocation_mode = direct`: movement value is consumed directly from `Financial Movement`
- `allocation_mode = allocated`: movement value must be distributed through `Allocation`
- allocated movements must not be double-counted directly plus in rateio

## 4. Workbook Mapping

The workbook template created for this phase uses:

- `README`
- `Resumo`
- `Dimensoes`
- `Orcamento_Mensal`
- `Movimentos`
- `Rateios`
- `Checks`

### 4.1 Source Sheets

- `Dimensoes`: structural master
- `Orcamento_Mensal`: monthly budget facts
- `Movimentos`: provisioned and realized launches
- `Rateios`: split allocations for `allocation_mode = allocated`

### 4.2 Control Sheets

- `Resumo`: monthly and annual executive visibility
- `Checks`: integrity checks before using the base as management truth

## 5. Minimum KPIs Supported

- approved monthly
- approved annual
- provisioned monthly
- provisioned annual
- realized monthly
- realized annual
- committed monthly
- committed annual
- monthly balance
- annual balance
- monthly status
- annual status

## 6. Filters Required

The base must support filtering by:

- year
- month
- asset
- cost center
- budget item
- movement type
- financial status
- allocation mode

## 7. Integrity Checks

Minimum control checks:

- direct movement without dimension
- allocated movement without matching allocation value
- allocation without source movement
- allocation without resolved movement type
- budget row without approved amount

## 8. Portal Evolution Guidance

When this workbook structure is absorbed by the portal, keep:

- structural master separated from monthly facts
- competence separated from payment date
- allocation support as a first-class concept
- approved budget as the official comparison baseline
- derived analytics generated by query/view, not by duplicated manual storage
